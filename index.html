About Me

Since 2016, when I built a board computer for an e-bike, I decided to dedicate my life to embedded systems development. Throughout my journey, I've had the opportunity to work on exciting projects, including cutting-edge eye-tracking glasses, various measurement and control systems, and even the development of a gaming console and a MIPS CPU on an FPGA. In addition to my studies at the Technical University of Vienna, I was fortunate to study at the renowned University of Tokyo and Portugal's Instituto Superior Técnico Lisboa. Despite all the experiences I've gained, my hunger for new knowledge remains stronger than ever.

WiFi LED Controller

Completion: 2025

An LED controller that can be accessed via its own WiFi access point or connected to a home network. The web UI, provided by the ESP32 microcontroller, allows to set static colors or to apply various patterns, like a rainbow mode. The UI focuses on simplicity and usability to allow children to control the LEDs as well. I assembled some of these LED controllers for family and friends, featuring a 1.5m LED strip. However, various lengths should be supported.

Technologies Used

  • ESP32 microcontroller
  • Rust (ESP-IDF)
  • WS2812 NeoPixel LED Strip
  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript
